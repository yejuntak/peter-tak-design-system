The clinical recommendation is a direction. The patient writes the plan. Patient-defined goals anchored to clinical guidance produce better adherence than clinician-mandated targets — because the patient chose the commitment.

Design rule: clinical recommendation = direction. Patient-defined goal = commitment. Show both — the source the direction came from, and the plan the patient authored — so the relationship between guidance and agency is legible.

Clinical context

Source · Vita Clinical Advisory Panel

Why activity, why now

Your fasting glucose has trended upward from 116 to 128 over 12 months — still in pre-diabetic territory, not yet diabetic. Patients with similar trends who reach ~150 minutes/week of moderate activity see an average 8–14 mg/dL decrease at their next test, often returning to the normal range without medication.

150 min/week is the long-term target. The patient-defined starting plan (60 min/week) builds the habit first; the system suggests cadence increases over time as the habit stabilizes.
