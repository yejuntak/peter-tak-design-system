Recommendations, with confidence.

Direction-not-directive copy, plain-language confidence tiers, transparent sources, and a three-action response (Accept · Modify · Dismiss) on every recommendation. Designed in 2019–2022 — same patterns I use for agentic AI today.

Design rule: never expose a raw confidence score. The system speaks three tiers — "Based on your results" (high), "Consider" (medium), "Ask your doctor about" (low). The phrase IS the tier — no freelancing new copy. Dismiss collects structured reasons that feed back into the model.

Why three tiers, not a percentage

Patients can't act on 0.84

A confidence score is technical jargon. The tier wraps the score in language a patient can use to weigh the recommendation.

Three tiers map to three responses

"Based on your results" → act. "Consider" → think. "Ask your doctor about" → escalate. The tier tells the patient what to do with the recommendation.

Thresholds live in the engine, not the UI

Designers don't pick which score maps to which tier — the team's recommendation engine does. The UI just consumes the tier.
